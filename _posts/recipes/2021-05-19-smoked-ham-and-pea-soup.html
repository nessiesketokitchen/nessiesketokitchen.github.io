I don't often cook or eat soup but this recipe changed all of that. I always double up the batch because I honestly can't get enough. The key to creating this moreish soup is to source good quality stock and smoked pork.

Prep time: 10 minutes
Cook time: 35 minutes
Serving: +-4x 400ml servings

Ingredients

  • 50g butter
  • 1 small onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 600g smoked pork rashers/chops
  • 2 cups chicken/pork stock
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 5 sprigs of mint
  • 3 cups frozen peas
  • 1 cup cream
  • ¼ cup defrosted peas for soup topping

Instructions


  1. Roughly chop the onion and garlic and pork rashers (keep one rasher aside for the topping).
  2. Fry the onion in the butter in a large pot for 5-7 minutes on a low/medium heat until soft and caramelised.
  3. Add the garlic and pork to the pot and fry for another 3 minutes.
  4. Add the stock, salt and mint and leave to simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. Cut the leftover pork rasher into lardons. Add to a fryin pan and fry until crispy. Add the ¼ of peas and warm through.
  6. Add the peas and cream to the pot and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Add to a blender/food processor and blend.
  8. Serve the soup with the pork lardons and peas.



Tip

Serve with thick buttered low carb bread or bread of choice.
Reheat the soup on a low heat to avoid the cream splitting.
When making a big batch do not add cream if you plan on freezing the soup, add the cream when reheating.
Add more stock if you don't like your soup as thick.
