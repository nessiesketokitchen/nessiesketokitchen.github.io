This is one my proudest recipes. This low carb and sugar-free cake is absolute heaven. The texture is soft, moist and light. You'll never need another cake recipe, I'd state my reputation on it.

Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 25-30 minutes
Serving: 1x 24cm cake

Ingredients: cake

  • 2 cups almond/macadamia flour
  • ⅓ cup whey powder
  • ½ cup erythritol
  • ½ tbsp psylium husk
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ cup boiling water
  • ¼ cup cocoa powder
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 5 extra-large eggs
  • 1 tsp vailla extract

Ingredients: chocolate mousse

  • Custard base
  • 1 cup sugar-free dark chocolate (+- 80g)
  • 1 cup cream

Ingredients: chocolate ganache

  • 1 cup cream
  • 1 ½ cups sugar-free dark chocolate (+- 120g)
  • ½ cup hazelnuts

Instructions: cake


  1. Preheat the oven to 180Â°C and line a 24cm cake tin.
  2. Combine all the dry ingredients except the cocoa powder in a mixing bowl.
  3. In another bowl mix together the boiling water and cocoa and leave for 2 minutes.
  4. Mix together the eggs, olive oil, vanilla and cocoa mixture and add to the dry ingredients.
  5. Pour the batter into the lined cake tin and bake for 25-30 minutes. Use a cake tester/skewer to test the doneness, if it comes out clean the cake is ready. If it is still wet: leave and test in 5 minute increments until done.
  6. Do not open the oven before 25 minutes to ensure the cake doesn't collapse.

Instructions: chocolate mousse


  1. Make the Custard.
  2. Break the chocolate into smaller pieces.
  3. Remove the custard from the heat when ready and stir in the chocolate until completely melted.
  4. Leave the custard to cool completely.
  5. Whip the cream to stiff peaks and fold into to custard.
  6. Put the mousse in the fridge and leave for a minimum of 1 hour.

Instructions: chocolate ganache


  1. Heat the cream.
  2. Roughly chop the hazelnuts.
  3. Break the chocolate into smaller pieces and stir into the cream until completely melted.
  4. Add the hazelnuts to the chocolate.
  5. Leave to cool at room temperature.

    6. Instructions: assemble


    1. Spread the ganache over each layer.
    2. Top each layer with mousse.
    3. Use the remaining mousse to decorate te cake on the outside.
    4. The mousse and ganache recipes are enough for a 3 layer cake.



      5. Tip

      CAKE:
      The quantity of the cake recipe is equal to one cake layer, multiply by the amount of layers you want for your cake.
      Do not let the cake batter stand for longer than 5 minutes (The batter becomes very thick and difficult to work with). If you don't have enough cake tins to bake at the same time rather make a new batch for each bake.
      This is a very thick batter due to the addition of the whey powder.
      The addition of boiling water to the cocoa powder is called BLOOMING, it helps release the flavour for a more intense cocoa flavour.

      CHOCOLATE MOUSSE:
      This mousse recipe is a little more involved but I promise you won't regret using a custard base.
      Make the mousse the day before to save time.
      Remove it 30 minutes before decorating from the fridge.

      CHOCOLATE GANACHE:
      Do not let the ganache harden completely (i.e., don't put it in the fridge) before spreading it over the cake layers.
