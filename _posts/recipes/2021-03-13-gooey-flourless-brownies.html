No eggs? No butter? No problem! These brownies are life changing, this is one of my favourite recipes. They are decadent, soft and super gooey. The secret Ingredient? MAYO!!! You'll never make your brownies any other way.

Prep time: 10 minutes
Cook time: 25 minutes
Serving: 1x 24cm square tin/casserole dish.

Ingredients: gooey brownies

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 2 tbsp whey powder
  • 1 tsp psyllium husk powder
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ¼ cup erythritol
  • 2 tbsp cocoa powder
  • ¼ cup boiling water
  • ¼ cup mayonnaise
  • ¾ cup cream

Ingredients: chocolate ganache

  • ¼ cup cream
  • 100g sugar-free chocolate

Instructions: brownies


  1. Preheat the oven to 180Â°C.
  2. Line the tin/dish with baking paper and grease as an extra precaution.
  3. Boil water.
  4. Combine all the dry ingredients (except the cocao powder) in a mixing bowl.
  5. Combine the cocoa and boiling water in a separate bowl.
  6. Add the mayonnaise and cream to the dry ingredients and mix.
  7. Add the cocoa powder and mix thoroughly.
  8. Pour the batter into the prepared tin/dish and bake for 25 minutes.
  9. Remove from the oven and let the brownies cool down in the tin/dish.
  10. Pour over the sugar-free chocolate ganache (see instructions below) and put into the fridge for 30 minutes to set.
  11. Remove the brownies from the fridge and use the baking paper to lift the brownies out from the tin/dish.
  12. Cut it into squares.
  13. Store in an airtight container. If you are stacking the brownies, layer each row with baking paper.
  14. Store it in the fridge (unless you have a very low room temperature).
  15. Remove the brownies, 10-15 minutes before eating, from the fridge.

Instructions: chocolate ganache


  1. Heat up the cream.
  2. Break the chocolate up and stir into the cream until completely melted.



Tip

These brownies don't contain any raw egg or flour so don't worry about under baking them.
Adding boiling water to the cocoa powder first, activates it and gives a richer chocolate taste: it's called blooming
The brownies will rise really high in the oven and then collapse when removed. This is because there are no eggs to form a structure but this is also what makes them super gooey.
Handle the brownies with care, they are more delicate than standard brownies because they don't contain eggs or flour.
