The perfect hearty meal for winter.

Ingredients

Yield: +- 2l soup.

  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 onion
  • 1kg pumpkin
  • 2.5 tbsp mother-in-law curry powder
  • 2.5 tbsp cumin
  • 2.5 tbsp ground ginger
  • 2 tbsp xylitol
  • 2 tbsp honey
    (this is optional as honey raises the carb count but this recipe makes at least 8 servings of which the carb count for 1 serving is low)
  • 3 ripe pears
  • 500ml stock of choice
    (I use chicken - it adds more flavour than vegetable stock)
  • salt - remember to season the layers

Recipe


  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C.
  2. Chop the garlic, onion and pumpkin.
  3. Fry the chopped garlic and onion in 1 tbsp butter till lightly browned and season.
  4. Add the second tbsp of butter and the chopped pumpkin.
  5. Add 2 tbsp curry spice, 2 tbsp cumin, 2 tbsp ginger and the xylitol and season again.
  6. Leave it to cook off on a low/medium heat (pumpkin contains a lot of water, you want it to evaporate but not burn so keep an eye on it).
  7. Peel and cut up the pears.
  8. Put the pears on a lined baking tray and add the remaing spices and honey and put it in the oven for 10 minutes.
  9. Once the pumpkin is sticky and not wet anymore, add the pears, the stock and season.
  10. Let simmer for 10 minutes to combine all the flavours.
  11. Blend and serve.

Tip

This goes well with a topping of crispy bacon.
