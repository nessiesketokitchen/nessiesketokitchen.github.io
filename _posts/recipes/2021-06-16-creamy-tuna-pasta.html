This creamy, rich and velvety smooth bowl of pasta isn't only quick and easy to make but the ingredients are already in most of our kitchens. Like most of my recipes, I was inspired by something I regularly ate growing up called tuna mate.

Prep time: 10 minutes
Cook time: 25 minutes
Serving: 2-4 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 small garlic clove
  • Handful chives (+-10g)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 cup cream
  • ¾ cup sour cream
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • ½ cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 2 cans tuna (170g)

Instructions


  1. Finely chop the garlic and chives. Add the butter to a medium sized pot and fry the garlic and chives on a low heat for 2 minutes
  2. Add the cream and sour cream to the pot and mix in the onion powder until completely combined.
  3. Turn up the heat (medium heat) and leave the mixture to cook for 5-10 minuntes (until it starts to thicken).
  4. Add the cheese to the sauce and mix thoroughly until completely melted.
  5. Drain the liquid from the tuna can and add the tuna to the the sauce.
  6. Leave it to cook for another 5-10 minutes.
  7. Serve with your choice of low-carb pasta and fresh chives.



Tip

I use a low-carb pasta made from seed and nut flours but this recipe is just as good served with vegetable noodles.
The sauce really thickens after you mix in the cheese but the tuna adds moisture. So only consider adding extra liquid (if it's still too thick) after you've added the tuna.
The serving suggestion is 2-4 because of the richness of the meal, some people might prefer a smaller portion.
