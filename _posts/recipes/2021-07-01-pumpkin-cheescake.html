Spicing up my original Cheesecake recipe with some winter flavour.

Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 60-120 minutes
Serving: 1x 24cm cake

Ingredients

Base
  • 1 ½ cup almond flour
  • ⅓ erythritol
  • ½ cup butter (extra to line the cake tin)
  • ¼ tsp each: ground ginger, cinnamon and all spice
  • ⅛ tsp each: ground cloves and nutmeg
Filling
  • 2 cups full fat cream cheese
  • ⅔ cup erythritol
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 2 cups pumpkin puree
  • 1 tbsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence
  • pinch of salt
  • 5 large eggs
Frosting
  • 3 tbsp cream cheese
  • 3 tbsp sour cream
  • 2 tbsp sugar-free syrup (maple flavour/caramel flavour)

Instructions: Base


  1. Preheat the oven to 160°C.
  2. Line the cake tin with baking paper and grease with butter as an added precaution.
  3. Melt the butter and combine it with the erythritol,almond flour and spices.
  4. Press the base mixture down into the cake tin and spread out evenly.
  5. Prebake the base for 10 minutes.

Instructions: Filling

  1. In a bowl whisk together all the ingredients except the eggs.
  2. Add the eggs and mix in by hand until completely blended.
  3. Pour the mixture into the cake tin and bake in a bain-marie for approximately 1-2 hours.
  4. Keep an eye on the Cheesecake. Check the Cheesecake after 1 hour. If it still isn't ready check it in 15 minute increments.
  5. The cake is ready when it's stiff but still slightly wobbly in the middle.
  6. Leave the cake to cool down in the tin.
  7. Refrigerate for a minimum of 2 hours before adding frosting on top.

Instructions: Frosting

  1. Mix together the ingredients and spread over cooled and set cake.


Tip

The cooking time of the Cheesecake will vary from oven to oven. That is why this recipe has such a wide range for the cooking time.
The reason I suggest mixing the eggs by hand is because you don't want the mixture to become overly frothy.
Bain-marie: A water-bath that assists with an even cook throughout the Cheesecake.
