Good on anything.

Ingredients

Yield: +- 600ml sauce (depends how much cooks off during the process).

  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp dijon mustard (dijon mustard contains no sugar)
  • 2 tbsp cumin
  • 2 tbsp coriander
  • 2 tbsp dried oreganum
  • 2 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp cinnamon
  • 1 tbsp ginger
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1-2 tbsp chilli powder (depending how hot you like it)
  • 175ml cup xylitol or any other sugar substitute
  • 1 can tomato puree/whole peeled tomatoes
  • 125ml water
  • 125ml vinegar (I prefer apple cider or white wine vinegar)
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp pepper

Recipe


  1. Fry all the spices and xylitol in a hot pan with the butter for about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the tomato puree, vinegar and water.
  3. Taste and add more salt if needed.
  4. Take the pot off the heat and leave it til it is cooled down before you transfer it to a container.
  5. Blend the mixture if you used whole peeled tomatoes.

Since all the spices are dried and the tomato puree is from a can it will last for quite a while.
