After consulting various traditional Greek recipes and adding my own personal touch, this recipe was born. I love playing around with popular dishes from other cultures and turning it into my own.

Prep time: 25 minutes
Cook time: 130 minutes
Serving: 4 (1x 24cm casserole dish)

Ingredients: eggplant layer

  • 2 large eggplants
  • salt
  • avocado oil for frying

Ingredients: meat layer

  • 25g butter
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 750g lamb or beef mince
  • 1 tin tomatoes
  • 1 cup lamb or beef stock
  • 1 tbsp erythritol
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • ½ tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp dried parsley
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Ingredients: béchamel layer

  • 450g cauliflower
  • 2 cups cream
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • 100g parmesan, grated

Instructions: eggplant layer


  1. Slice the eggplants into 2cm thick rounds.
  2. Salt the rounds and leave for 20 minutes.
  3. Rinse off the salt and pat dry.
  4. Fry the eggplant rounds (on a high heat) in avocado oil for 3 minutes each side or until golden brown.
  5. Layer the eggplant on the bottom of the casserole dish.

Instructions: meat layer


  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C.
  2. Finely chop the onions and mince the garlic.
  3. Melt the butter in a large sauce-pan and add the onions. Leave the onions to fry on a medium, low heat until soft and golden brown.
  4. Add the garlic and mince and fry for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add the rest of the ingredients and leave uncovered to simmer for 40-60 minutes
  6. The longer you leave the mince to simmer, the richer and more developed the sauce will be.
  7. Add the mince on top of the eggplant layer.

Instructions: béchamel layer


  1. Break the cauliflower up into small florets and spread out on a baking tray.
  2. Roast in the oven on 200°C for 15-20 minutes (unil it starts to brown).
  3. Turn the oven down to 180°C.
  4. When the the cauliflower is ready combine it with the rest of the ingredients and blend.
  5. Pour the béchamel on top of the mince layer.
  6. Bake in the oven for 30 minutes°C on 180°C.
  7. Turn the heat up to 200°C and set to grill, leave for another 5-10 minutes until golden brown on the top.


Tip

You can cook the mince in your slow cooker for 6-8 hours.
For the best results use a good quality stock.
The cultivation of eggplants have changed and they don't need to be salted as much but it's a step I still like to add in case the eggplant is bitter.
