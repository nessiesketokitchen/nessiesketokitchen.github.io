I grew up eating and loving of baked beans but with all the added sugar in commercially produced baked beans, it's something I had to give up. Legumes are a great source of fiber but the problem with baked beans specifically is all the sugar found in the sauce. So I set out to recreate one of my favourite childhood meals.

Prep time: 5 minutes
Cook time: 17 minutes
Serving: equal to 1 store bought can of baked beans

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup sugar-free tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup stock
  • 1 tbsp erythritol
  • 3 drops liquid smoke
  • pinch of salt
  • 1 can butter beans, drained

Instructions


  1. In a small pot, combine all the ingredients except the butter beans. Cook on a medium heat until the sauce starts to simmer (1-2 minutes).
  2. Add the butter beans and cook uncovered for 15 minutes or until the sauce has thickened.
  3. Remove from the heat and serve warm or leave to cool down.



Tip

I use butter beans because they are equal to 7g net carbs per 100g. Just keep in mind what else you're consuming if you want to stay in ketosis.
Beans are a great option for carb cycling. I never cycle above 50g net carbs which means I might not follow a ketogenic plan everyday but I still remain low carb.
